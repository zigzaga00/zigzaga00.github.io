about our

mission

Girls are brilliant at computing — creative, intelligent, and full of amazing ideas. But girls only make up 17% of A-level Computing students! We're here to help change that. In our sessions, girls take the lead on projects that matter to them - learning in a safe, supportive space - following their interests together and growing in confidence as digital creators and changemakers.

welcome aboard

Join Us

Come and JOIN THE ZIG-ZAG CYBER-LAB — a FUN, FREE and FRIENDLY computing club for GIRLS ONLY where every girl is welcome, no matter how much (or how little!) experience she has.

We meet SUNDAY AFTERNOONS from 2–4pm at the TIDDINGTON COMMUNITY CENTRE. Girls aged 7 AND UP can bring a laptop or tablet if they have one — but don’t worry if not! You can easily share with others!

The most important thing is that you're ready to EXPLORE, CREATE, and HAVE FUN! Our venue has a BIG SCREEN, PROJECTOR and SOUND SYSTEM, so we can WATCH and CELEBRATE each other’s DIGITAL CREATIONS — from DIGITAL ART, ANIMATIONS, GAMES and WEBSITES to iMovie FILMS and TRAILERS which have been FILMED ON THE TCC'S STAGE!

There’s DRINKING WATER and TOILETS available - no need to worry about that!

So, if you LOVE MAKING THINGS, being CREATIVE with COMPUTERS, or want to HELP CHANGE THE WORLD with TECH, all while MAKING NEW FRIENDS in a RELAXED and SUPPORTIVE space, you are WELCOME to COME ALONG and JOIN US!

explore our

heritage

barbara rogoff

Barbara Rogoff shows us that learning doesn't happen in isolation—it happens through shared experiences, in real communities, and with purpose. In the Zig-Zag Cyber-Lab, her ideas come to life as children learn side by side, helping one another. Here, learning is collaborative, empowering, and rooted in the real world.

read more

maria montessori

Maria Montessori believed that children thrive when they are free to follow their curiosity in a carefully prepared, respectful environment. Her approach inspires the Zig-Zag Cyber-Lab to be a focussed, creative space where learners choose their own paths and work at their own pace. We believe that with trust and freedom, all children flourish.

read more

grace hopper

Grace Hopper was a visionary computer scientist who believed that thinking differently and questioning limits were the keys to progress. At the Zig-Zag Cyber-Lab, we follow her example: encouraging bold ideas, supporting fearless experimentation, and championing inclusion to spark change, open doors and empower girls to shape the future.
